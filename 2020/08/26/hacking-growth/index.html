Hacking Growth Notes

Posted on 2020-08-26 Edited on 2025-04-04

Overview

In the modern business we are increasingly discovering the inadequacy of traditional marketing methods. Even in technology companies, often we are still seeing marketing being separated from product development. This separation means a lack of interaction between the development team and the marketing team, thus a disconnection between the customers and the people developing their product. The book Hacking Growth analises the disadvantages of this, and introduces the Hacking Growth method, which is the act of combining marketing with product development, and applying agile software development process to improving the customer experience. This different way of marketing the product is more customer experience oriented, and aims to scientifically improve the product performance and thus achieve growth, through observing customers. This method has been used with proven potential to achieve explosive growth in some of the biggest companies today such as Facebook, Twitter, Dropbox, eBay and Paypal, just to name a few.

How to start Hacking Growth

The process of hacking growth is applicable to every firm, as it is ultimately a change from developing a piece of technology and pursuading the public to like it, to tailoring the product to the needs of its customers, whether the group of customers are the intended target of the product or not. For any company to set up this strategy, the following few steps are needed:

Setting up a growth team

The growth team should either be a separate team working with various products in the company (this is more common for smaller firms or start-ups) or integrated into a product team. The team can be permanent or temporary, meaning they could either be a long term part of the company or a specialist team set up for a particular project or even part of a project. The team should contain the following members:
1. Growth manager - The leader of the team, responsible for fascilitating the growth hacking process. The growth manager will determine the testing process and tempo, and make sure the team is achieving its objective. The manager will also lead the weekly meetings. No matter what background the manager came from, the role needs to also cover work of a product manager and a data scientist. The growth manager will also choose a monthly core performance index to pay attention to, and make sure the team measure the correct data to condense the results of the tests conducted.
2. Product manager - The product manager is responsible for helping the company to understand the needs of the customers.
3. Software engineers - Responsible for bringing about the changes and actuallly implementing the tests. However they are not only technical staff, who listens to the product team and write code. The software engineers are also an important part of the growth team because they have the innovative mindset and technical skills which can often bring very good growth ideas.
4. Marketing - The mix of engineers and marketing staff can bring about effective new ideas.
5. Data scientists - they will be responsible for collecting data, pre-processing and conducting the analysis which indicate changes or effects on the key performance index. This can’t be an intern who only knows how to work Google analytics, as these software solutions may only offer shallow insights and can’t work with data coming from different sources like customer stalking data.
